Mental & Physical Health

Fitness, in all of its many forms, has helped me become a better version of myself. As I improved my physical health, I found that my mental state and sense of self followed suit. I was 16 years old, low in self-confidence, & generally unhappy with my life. My insecurities fostered constant anxiety, which I allowed to hold me back from doing the things I wanted to. I wasn't comfortable in my own skin and was living life through my inhibitions, not my passions.

In fitness, I found the resources to grow into the person I wanted to be & gained the confidence I had been looking for. This newfound confidence came hand in hand with humbling experiences, never letting myself cross the line into arrogance. Through this cycle of growth & humility, I solidified my belief that we should all strive to improve ourselves and empower those around us to to the same. Nothing actualizes confidence more than knowing you have instilled it in others. This lifesytle choice is an iterative one; there are ups & downs, accomidations need to be made for changes in my life, and there's constant application of lessons-learned to life outside the gym. I choose fitness because it has, & continues to, make me a better version of myself.
